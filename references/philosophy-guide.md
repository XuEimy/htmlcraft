# Design Philosophy Guide (Layer 1)

## What Is a Visual Thesis?

A visual thesis is a single sentence that defines the world the page inhabits. It is not
a description of features ("clean layout with blue buttons"). It is a declaration of
identity ("a laboratory notebook: precise, dense, clinical, every mark intentional").

A good visual thesis answers: "If this page were a physical space, what would it feel like
to walk into?"

## How to Construct a Visual Thesis

A visual thesis has three components:

### 1. Metaphor (the world)
What physical or cultural reference anchors the design?

Examples:
- Print editorial (newspaper, magazine, art book)
- Architecture (museum, factory, chapel, greenhouse)
- Material (glass, stone, paper, neon, wood)
- Era (Swiss modernism, Y2K, Bauhaus, 1970s California)
- Environment (laboratory, gallery, workshop, cockpit)

### 2. Tension (the energy)
Every strong design lives on a spectrum between two opposing forces.
Pick where your page sits:

- Dense ←→ Spacious
- Loud ←→ Quiet
- Warm ←→ Cold
- Organic ←→ Geometric
- Playful ←→ Serious
- Raw ←→ Polished
- Static ←→ Kinetic

### 3. Material Quality (the texture)
How does the page "feel" to the eye?

- Ink on paper (matte, absorbent, high contrast)
- Light on glass (transparent, refractive, luminous)
- Metal on dark (sharp, reflective, precise)
- Paint on canvas (textured, imperfect, human)
- Pencil on grid paper (structured, provisional, technical)

## Thesis → Design Decisions

Once the thesis exists, every design decision is a deduction:

| Visual Thesis | → Whitespace | → Type Weight | → Color Range | → Motion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Print editorial: dense, type-heavy, no wasted space" | Compressed | Heavy/Black | Limited (2–3) | Minimal |
| "Museum lobby: every object has room to breathe" | Generous | Light/Regular | Muted neutrals | Slow, subtle |
| "Neon machine: dark, luminous, precise" | Moderate | Medium/Bold | Dark + 1 bright accent | Glowing, pulsing |
| "Handmade letter: warm, imperfect, personal" | Moderate-generous | Medium | Warm earth tones | Gentle, organic |
| "Swiss poster: geometric, structured, graphic" | Structured grid | Bold/Black | Primary colors | None or snap |
| "Brutalist concrete: raw, honest, confrontational" | Dense | Heavy/Black | Monochrome + 1 signal | Harsh or none |

## Common Mistakes

### No thesis
"I want it to look modern and clean" is not a thesis. It's a preference.
Modern and clean HOW? Like a Scandinavian furniture catalog? Like a Japanese tea room?
Like a Tesla dashboard? These produce completely different designs.

### Contradictory thesis
"Minimal but information-rich" requires resolution. You can have dense information presented
with minimal decoration (editorial style), or you can have sparse information with lots of
whitespace (museum style). You cannot have both. Force a choice.

### Thesis too vague
"Professional and trustworthy" could describe a law firm, a bank, a hospital, or a SaaS tool.
Add the metaphor: "Professional like a well-bound legal brief: structured, serif-heavy, no
frivolity" — now every design decision has a compass.

## Thesis Templates

When the user gives vague input, use these templates to construct a thesis:

| User Says | Suggested Thesis |
|---|---|
| "高级 / high-end / luxury" | "A curated gallery: restraint is luxury, every element precisely placed, nothing competes for attention" |
| "科技感 / techy / futuristic" | "A control interface: dark field, luminous data, engineered precision, function dictates form" |
| "简洁 / clean / simple" | "An architect's portfolio: white walls, sharp edges, typography carries the entire weight" |
| "活泼 / playful / fun" | "A toy store window: bold colors, chunky shapes, everything invites interaction" |
| "专业 / professional" | "A Swiss annual report: structured grid, clear hierarchy, authoritative type, zero decoration" |
| "温暖 / warm / friendly" | "A neighborhood café: natural materials, soft lighting, rounded edges, handwritten touches" |
| "大气 / grand / impressive" | "A concert hall lobby: scale creates awe, generous proportions, restraint in detail" |
| "像苹果 / apple-like" | "Product as monument: the object speaks, everything else steps back, precision in every pixel" |
| "酷 / cool / edgy" | "A brutalist gallery: raw surfaces, confrontational scale, no comfort, no compromise" |

## Validating a Thesis

Before proceeding to Layer 2, check:

1. Can you visualize a physical space from the thesis? (If no, it's too abstract)
2. Does the thesis exclude at least half of possible design approaches? (If no, it's too vague)
3. Can you derive whitespace, type weight, and color range directly from it? (If no, add material quality)
4. Would two different designers reading this thesis produce recognizably similar designs? (If no, add more specificity)
